# Template — Clean Water Act §505 Notice of Intent to Sue

> **DRAFT SCAFFOLD — NOT A FILING.** This template encodes the *required elements* of a Clean Water Act citizen-suit notice. A defective notice can get the entire later lawsuit dismissed regardless of merit, so every element below must be present and accurate, and the whole document must be reviewed and signed by a licensed attorney before service. This template is a starting structure, not legal advice. See `../DISCLAIMER.md`.

## Why the formality matters

The 60-day notice is a jurisdictional prerequisite to a Clean Water Act citizen suit under 33 U.S.C. §1365 (CWA §505). Courts have dismissed suits where the notice failed to give the recipient enough specific information to identify and correct the violations. The point of the notice is to let the alleged violator and the regulators act *before* litigation. Draft it to actually serve that function.

- [ ] The activity alleged to constitute the violation
- [ ] The location of the violation (facility, outfall, water body)
- [ ] The date(s) or date range of the violation

## Deadline note

The Clean Water Act citizen-suit provision requires that this notice be given **60 days before** filing suit. That is a fact for counsel to verify and a clock for counsel to track; this software does not track it. Serving suit before the period runs can defeat the case.

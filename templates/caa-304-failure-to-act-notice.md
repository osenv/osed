# Template — Clean Air Act §304(a)(2) Notice of Intent to Sue (Failure to Perform a Nondiscretionary Duty)

> **DRAFT SCAFFOLD — NOT A FILING.** This template encodes the *required elements* of a Clean Air Act §304(a)(2) citizen-suit notice to the EPA Administrator for failure to perform a nondiscretionary act or duty under the Clean Air Act. A defective notice can get the entire later lawsuit dismissed regardless of merit, so every element below must be present and accurate, and the whole document must be reviewed and signed by a licensed attorney before service. This template is a starting structure, not legal advice. See `../DISCLAIMER.md`.

## Why the formality matters

The 60-day notice to the Administrator is a jurisdictional prerequisite to a Clean Air Act §304(a)(2) citizen suit under 42 U.S.C. §7604 (CAA §304). Courts have dismissed suits where the notice failed to give the agency enough specific information to identify the duty and act on it. The point of the notice is to let the agency perform the duty *before* litigation. Draft it to actually serve that function.

## Deadline note

The §304(b)(2) 60-day clock is a fact for counsel to verify and a clock for counsel to track; this software does not track it. The §7412(i)(3)(A)/(f)(4) or §7413(a) immediate-suit exception may remove the 60-day wait — confirm with counsel.

## Consolidated attorney flags (carry forward into the Drafting agent's output)

- Supply and verify the nondiscretionary duty and its deadline from the agency record; do not allege unsupported dates or non-performance.

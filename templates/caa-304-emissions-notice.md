# Template — Clean Air Act §304(a)(1) Notice of Intent to Sue (Emission Violation)

> **DRAFT SCAFFOLD — NOT A FILING.** This template encodes the *required elements* of a Clean Air Act §304(a)(1) citizen-suit notice against an alleged violator of an emission standard, limitation, or order. A defective notice can get the entire later lawsuit dismissed regardless of merit, so every element below must be present and accurate, and the whole document must be reviewed and signed by a licensed attorney before service. This template is a starting structure, not legal advice. See `../DISCLAIMER.md`.

## Why the formality matters

The 60-day notice is a jurisdictional prerequisite to a Clean Air Act §304(a)(1) citizen suit under 42 U.S.C. §7604 (CAA §304). Courts have dismissed suits where the notice failed to give the recipient enough specific information to identify and correct the violations. The point of the notice is to let the alleged violator and the regulators act *before* litigation. Draft it to actually serve that function.

- [ ] The activity alleged to constitute the violation
- [ ] The location of the violation (facility, emission unit / stack)
- [ ] The date(s) or date range of the violation
- [ ] Whether the violation is alleged to be ongoing or recurring `[⚠ ATTORNEY: the ongoing-violation judgment is yours, not the agent's]`
- [ ] The applicable permit / standard identifier(s) (e.g., Title V permit number, NSPS/NESHAP subpart)
- [ ] Notice served on **the EPA Administrator, the State air agency, and the alleged violator** `[⚠ ATTORNEY: confirm the full current service list and addresses per 40 C.F.R. Part 54, and the certified-mail/postmark service rules]`
- [ ] Whether the **§7412(i)(3)(A)/(f)(4) or §7413(a) immediate-suit exception** applies (i.e., whether the 60-day wait is even required) `[⚠ ATTORNEY: assess immediate-suit eligibility for the specific standard at issue]`
- [ ] Whether a **diligent-prosecution bar** applies (EPA/State already prosecuting) `[⚠ ATTORNEY: confirm no civil action has been commenced and is being diligently prosecuted]`
- [ ] Signature of/over review by the responsible attorney

## Deadline note

The §304(b)(1) 60-day clock is a fact for counsel to verify and a clock for counsel to track; this software does not track it. The §7412(i)(3)(A)/(f)(4) or §7413(a) immediate-suit exception may remove the 60-day wait and the diligent-prosecution bar may foreclose the suit — confirm both with counsel.

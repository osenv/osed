# The disclaimer — read this first

OSED exists to lower one barrier: the cost of producing a well-formed first draft of an
environmental-litigation instrument. Where a community or a small public-interest organization
has a real concern but no drafting capacity, OSED turns proven instruments into something they
can prepare and bring to a lawyer. That is the whole promise — and its limit.

The premise the whole project rests on:

> **OSED drafts; a licensed attorney decides.**

OSED produces *scaffolding*, not filings. Everything it generates is a marked **DRAFT** that a
licensed attorney in the relevant jurisdiction must review before it is sent, served, or filed.
Using OSED creates no attorney-client relationship, and nothing it produces is legal advice. The
software does not decide whether you have a case, whether to sue, or whether a claim is sound —
it surfaces those questions, with the relevant law attached, and stops.

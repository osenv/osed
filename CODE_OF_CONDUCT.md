# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

OSED is a public-interest project. We are building tools meant to widen access
to environmental and public-health law. We expect everyone who participates —
contributors, reviewers, maintainers, and users seeking help — to act in a way
consistent with that purpose.

## Expected behavior

- Treat others with respect, especially across differences in legal expertise,
  technical background, and lived experience. A retired litigator and a
  first-time community organizer are equally welcome and equally valued here.
- Give and receive feedback constructively. Critique the instrument, not the
  person.
- Flag your own uncertainty. Saying "I'm not sure this element carries legal
  weight" makes the project safer.
- Keep the mission in view: these tools exist to help people use the law, not
  to weaponize it.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or demeaning conduct of any kind.
- Using the project, its issues, or its tools to facilitate harassment,
  bad-faith filings, or any use contrary to the public-interest purpose stated
  in DISCLAIMER.md.
- Pressuring contributors to weaken the safeguards (DRAFT banners, attorney
  flags, refusal behaviors) that keep the tools responsible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions, issues, and comments
that violate this code, and may temporarily or permanently bar contributors
whose behavior is harmful or contrary to the project's purpose.

Report concerns to the maintainers through the repository's designated contact
channel. Reports will be handled with discretion.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted in spirit from common open-source community
standards, tailored to the public-interest nature of this project.
